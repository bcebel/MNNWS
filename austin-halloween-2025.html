Austin Halloween & Día de los Muertos Guide 2025

Austin Halloween 2025 is here! This guide highlights the top haunted houses, live music, and family-friendly Halloween events. We also cover the vibrant Día de los Muertos celebrations happening around the city.

Live Music & Festivals

Levitation Halloween Freakend feat. Stereolab

Where: The Far Out Lounge, 8504 S Congress Ave

When: Oct. 31, 5:00 PM

Legendary UK band Stereolab headlines a two-day psychedelic and garage rock mini-festival. All ages.

Levitation Halloween Freakend (Night 2)

Where: The Far Out Lounge, 8504 S Congress Ave

When: Nov. 1, 4:00 PM

The second night features Machine Girl, Clown Core, and Black Moth Super Rainbow.

Austin Haunted Houses

House of Torment

Where: 2632 Ridgepoint Dr, Austin

When: Select nights through Nov. 9

A nationally recognized haunted house with brand-new, elaborate attractions like "Blood Stained" and "Cursed: Pharaoh's Tomb."

Scream Hollow Wicked Halloween Park

Where: 149 Split Rail Ln, Smithville

When: Select nights through Nov. 2

Located on 20 acres, this park features five distinct haunted attractions, live music, and themed bars.

UNDEAD: Haunted House of Dances

Where: East Side Performing Arts, 979 Springdale Rd

When: Starts Oct. 23

An adults-only immersive dance show that blends performance art with a haunted house experience.

Movies & Performances

Panic at the Paramount

Where: Paramount Theater, 713 Congress Ave

When: Select dates including Oct. 31

Catch classic horror thrillers like 'Halloween' and 'Pet Sematary' in the historic Paramount Theater.

Candlelight: A Haunted Evening of Halloween Classics

Where: St. David's Episcopal Church, 301 E 8th St

When: Oct. 30

The Austin Camerata String Quartet performs eerie classics like “Thriller” and the 'Stranger Things' theme by candlelight.

Halloween Children's Concert

Where: The Long Center, 701 W Riverside Dr

When: Oct. 26, 3:00 PM

The Austin Symphony Orchestra's annual, all-ages concert with themed music and costumes.

Family-Friendly Austin Halloween Events

Pumpkin Nights

Where: Pioneer Farms, 10621 Pioneer Farms Dr

When: Sep. 18 – Oct. 31

A half-mile trail featuring over 7,500 hand-carved pumpkins in themed "Lands."

The Nightmare Before Christmas Light Trail

Where: Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center

When: Sep. 25 – Nov. 30

An immersive, 8,300+ sq ft light trail experience based on the classic movie.

Spooktacular at the Bullock Museum

Where: Bullock Texas State History Museum

When: Oct. 24, 5:00 PM – 8:00 PM

A free, family-friendly event with slimy science activities, music, and a scavenger hunt.

Boo at the Zoo

Where: Austin Zoo, 10808 Rawhide Trail

When: Oct. 24 & 25, 5:45 PM

The zoo transforms with festive lights, a Haunted Train ride, and a Haunted House.

Bars & Pop-Ups

Black Lagoon Pop-Up Bar

Where: King Bee Lounge, 1900 E 12th St

When: Oct. 1 – Oct. 31

An international, goth, and horror-inspired pop-up bar with themed cocktails.

Twisted Carnival Pop-Up Bar

Where: Gibson Street Bar, 1109 S Lamar Blvd

When: Oct. 1 – Nov. 2

Gibson Street Bar’s annual Halloween pop-up with carnival decor and twisted cocktails.

Día de los Muertos Events

Viva La Vida Festival & Parade

Where: Downtown Austin, starts at 4th & Congress

When: Oct. 25, 12:00 PM - 6:00 PM

Austin's largest Día de los Muertos event with a grand procession, art activities, and live music. Free and family-friendly.

Día de los Muertos at Waterloo Park

Where: Moody Amphitheater at Waterloo Park

When: Nov. 1 & 2, 6:00 PM

A two-night celebration with a community ofrenda, music, and an artisan marketplace. One of the best Austin Halloween events.
